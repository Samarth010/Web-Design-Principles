Web Testing

  • Rocket Surgery Made Easy by Steve Krug

  • After Developing the webpage its very important that you do the usability testing because it’s very important. It doesn’t require any tester for usability testing you can do the usability testing yourself.

  • • Do it yourself Usability Testing is also known as Discount Usability Testing.

  • Chapter 1 : What DIY Testing Is

    1. Quantitative Tests – They simply require insights to improve what you’re building, proves things by measuring others and are rigorous.

    2. The problems which are easy to find are the serious ones, and the “hidden” ones will be removed after you have proper resources.

    3. You can never know why people are doing that but web analytics will let you know what people are doing.

  • Chapter 3 : A Plan You Can Follow

    1. You can schedule a testing one morning in one month, it shortness simplifies recruiting.

    2. Do not take breaks while testing, do it back-to-back in a half day so a debrief can be conducted with fresh minds.

  • Chapter 4 : The Hardest Part Is Starting Early Enough

    1. If you can benefit from something, it’s the thing which is in the worse shape.

    2. Learn from the mistakes from the available sites, for the similar feature which you want to implement in your site.

    3. Do not ask someone for the opinion or feedback of what you implemented only ask what they think about it.

    4. Usability problems can be introduced by visual design so test before you implement.

  • Chapter 5 : Who To Test And How To Find Them

    1. Recruit participants with domain knowledge who have some experience, do not recruit first-timers for this task.

    2. You need three participants; any more yields diminishing returns, increases tedium, and surfaces more nits that make triaging difficult.

    3. If you provide invitation testing, make sure to choose persons who can check of you really met the qualifications and are comfortable talking-out-loud.

    4. Do not use participants again in later testing because a fresh eye can make a difference.

  • Chapter 6 : Picking Tasks And Writing Scenarios

    1. Have a tester also do the task to the competitor site to know the difference from your site.

    2. Use simple words so that the tester can easily do the testing rather to find the meaning of the words.

    3. No need for too much instructions in website.

  • Chapter 7 : Why You Should Use Boring Checklists/h2>

    1. You can give a full attention to a tester if you omit the details of the testing you have made

  • Chapter 8 : Conducting The Test Session

    1. Facilitator shouldn’t tell participants what to do but instead keep them testing on their own and have them verbalise the thoughts.

    2. Screen Resolution should be such as the average user use not as of developer.

    3. If the tester is not doing the task well and you aren’t learning anything more move to the next task.

    4. User generally don’t know exactly what they need because they aren’t designers.

    5. Don’t make such functions or popups which can interrupt while reading or making progress.

  • Chapter 9 : What Observers Should Look For

    1. Live tests are more compelling than recordings and also benefit from the shared group experience and comparing observations.

    2. Observers don’t have passive role they should actively participate in testing and make notes of three usability problems they saw in the session and suggest this to facilitator.

    3. If the development is done late then the testing team will face problems and will not have any time to fix problems.

  • Chapter 10 : Comparing Notes And Deciding What To Fix

    1. Focus on most serious problems, because you should be having more usability problems than resources to fix.

    2. At least observe and list ten worst problems and discuss the simple fixes in the debriefing and make the fixes within the month.

  • Chapter 11 : Why Doing Less Is The Best Way To Fix Things

    1. Don’t ignore the smallest and simplest changes because they matter, you can do implementation later.

    2. Try a simple solution first, if it doesn’t work try a stronger version of it and if that also don’t work again try another one before redesign.

    3. Anything distracting should be removed.

  • Chapter 12 : Common Problems And How To Fix Them

    1. If someone who is lost from the start on your website will stay lost, don’t let too many of them add disoriented bits.

    2. The important part on your website should really stand out.

  • Chapter 13 : Playing Nicely With Others

    1. If you want buy in make them observe the live usability tests, it much better than ROI arguments.

  • Chapter 14 : Remote Testing

    1. You can’t ask questions through remote testing, but it’s cheap and helpful.

    2. You can only perform remote testing after you have done some in-person tests.
